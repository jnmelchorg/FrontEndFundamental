Article 1

"Per aspera ad astra" - Meaning "Through hardships to the stars." This phrase is often used to convey the idea that success and greatness can be achieved through overcoming difficulties and challenges.

Article 2

"Carpe diem" - Meaning "Seize the day." This phrase encourages individuals to make the most out of the present moment, to embrace opportunities, and to live life to the fullest without procrastination or hesitation.

Article 3

"Veni, vidi, vici" - Meaning "I came, I saw, I conquered." This phrase is famously attributed to Julius Caesar, referring to his swift and decisive victory at the Battle of Zela in 47 BC. It is often used to express a triumphant or successful outcome achieved with determination and efficiency.
